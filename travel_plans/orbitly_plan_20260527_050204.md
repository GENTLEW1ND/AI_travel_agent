## Itinerary
Given the information provided, I'll create a 7-day trip to Japan. Since the flight results don't directly relate to a trip from a specific location to Japan, I'll focus on planning the itinerary within Japan itself, assuming you've already arranged your flights to and from Japan.

### Day 1: Arrival in Tokyo
- **Morning:** Arrive at one of Tokyo's airports (Narita or Haneda). Purchase a Suica or Pasmo card for convenient travel on public transportation.
- **Afternoon:** Check into a hostel in Shinjuku, such as Anshin Oyado Shinjuku or Wise Owl Hostel River Tokyo, for a central location.
- **Evening:** Explore Shinjuku. Visit the Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building for a free observation deck with great city views. Then, experience the vibrant nightlife of Shinjuku, perhaps in the Golden Gai or Kabukicho areas.

### Day 2: Tokyo
- **Morning:** Visit the Meiji Shrine, a serene Shinto shrine located in a tranquil forested area.
- **Afternoon:** Head to Harajuku to see the fashionable youth culture and try some crepes or cotton candy. Then, walk down to Shibuya to see the famous Shibuya Crossing and take a stroll around the trendy Shibuya area.
- **Evening:** Enjoy dinner at a traditional izakaya in the Shibuya area.

### Day 3: Tokyo to Kyoto
- **Morning:** Travel to Kyoto by bullet train (Shinkansen). Check into a hostel like Guesthouse U-En or Ark Hostel.
- **Afternoon:** Visit the Fushimi Inari Shrine, famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates that form a tunnel up the mountain.
- **Evening:** Explore the Gion district, known for its geisha culture, and enjoy a traditional Kyoto-style dinner.

### Day 4: Kyoto
- **Morning:** Visit the Kinkaku-ji Temple (Golden Pavilion) and the Ryoan-ji Temple, famous for its Zen garden.
- **Afternoon:** Stroll through the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove and visit the Tenryu-ji Temple.
- **Evening:** Consider a traditional tea ceremony experience.

### Day 5: Kyoto to Osaka
- **Morning:** Travel to Osaka. Check into a hostel in the city center.
- **Afternoon:** Visit the Osaka Castle and the surrounding park. Then, explore the Dotonbori area, known for its neon lights, street food, and unique entertainment.
- **Evening:** Enjoy the nightlife in Shinsaibashi, with its many bars and clubs.

### Day 6: Osaka
- **Morning:** Visit the Umeda Sky Building for panoramic views of the city. Then, explore the Kuromon Ichiba Market for a taste of local food.
- **Afternoon:** Head to Universal Studios Japan for a day of thrills and entertainment.
- **Evening:** Return to Dotonbori for dinner and more exploration.

### Day 7: Departure from Osaka
- **Morning:** Last-minute shopping for souvenirs or visiting any last sights.
- **Afternoon:** Depart for the airport and your return flight, bringing back memories of your fantastic trip to Japan.

### Hotel/Hostel Recommendations:
- **Tokyo:** Wise Owl Hostel River Tokyo or Anshin Oyado Shinjuku
- **Kyoto:** Guesthouse U-En or Ark Hostel
- **Osaka:** Consider booking through Expedia for the best options within your budget.

### Transportation:
- Use the Shinkansen for inter-city travel (Tokyo to Kyoto to Osaka).
- For intra-city travel, use the Suica or Pasmo card on subways and buses.

This itinerary provides a mix of culture, history, entertainment, and relaxation, making for a well-rounded trip to Japan. Enjoy your travels!
