## Itinerary
Based on your query, I've created a 7-day travel itinerary for a trip to Mumbai from Delhi. Since you're looking for affordable accommodation options, I've considered the hostel options provided in the results.

**Day 1: Arrival in Mumbai**

* Take a morning flight from Delhi to Mumbai (flight duration: approximately 2.5 hours)
* Check-in at a hostel in the Colaba or Bandra area, such as Backpacker Cowies or Indie Stays - BKC ( prices start from $6)
* Spend the afternoon exploring the Colaba Causeway, a popular shopping and dining street
* Visit the Gateway of India, a iconic landmark in Mumbai

**Day 2: Mumbai City Tour**

* Start the day with a visit to the Haji Ali Dargah, a historic mosque located on an islet off the coast of Mumbai
* Take a taxi or bus to the Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (CST), a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the busiest railway stations in India
* Visit the Marine Drive, also known as the "Queen's Necklace", a scenic promenade along the Arabian Sea
* Explore the street food scene in Mumbai and try some local delicacies like vada pav or pani puri

**Day 3: Bollywood and Beaches**

* Take a guided tour of a Bollywood film studio, such as Film City or the Bollywood Studio Tour ( prices start from $20)
* Visit the Juhu Beach, a popular spot for locals and tourists alike
* Relax on the beach and try some street food or local snacks
* In the evening, head to the Bandra area and explore the trendy bars and restaurants

**Day 4: Elephanta Caves and Harbour**

* Take a ferry to the Elephanta Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to ancient rock-cut temples ( prices start from $10)
* Explore the caves and learn about the history and significance of the site
* In the evening, head to the Harbour area and take a stroll along the waterfront
* Try some fresh seafood at one of the local restaurants

**Day 5: Shopping and Markets**

* Spend the day shopping at some of Mumbai's famous markets, such as the Chor Bazaar or the Linking Road market
* Visit the Phoenix Market City Mall, a large shopping mall with a variety of international and local brands
* Try some local street food or snacks at the mall or in the surrounding areas

**Day 6: Historical Landmarks**

* Visit the Victoria Terminus, a historic railway station and a UNESCO World Heritage Site
* Explore the nearby CST Museum, which showcases the history of the railways in India
* Visit the Rajabai Clock Tower, a historic clock tower located in the heart of the city
* Take a stroll through the Horniman Circle, a beautiful garden surrounded by historic buildings

**Day 7: Departure**

* Spend the morning shopping for last-minute souvenirs or exploring a local market
* Check-out of the hostel and head to the airport for your return flight to Delhi

**Budget Breakdown:**

* Flights: $100 - $200 (depending on the airline and time of booking)
* Accommodation: $6 - $20 per night (depending on the hostel and availability)
* Food and transportation: $10 - $20 per day (depending on individual preferences and exchange rates)
* Activities and entrance fees: $10 - $20 per person (depending on the activity and location)

Total estimated budget for the 7-day trip: $300 - $600 per person

Note: The budget breakdown is an estimate and can vary depending on individual preferences and exchange rates.
